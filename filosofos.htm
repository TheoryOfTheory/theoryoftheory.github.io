Why?
* The assessment of where the best / most important philosophical work was being done during a given period tend to correlate quite strongly with our assessment of where the best / most important scientific work is being done during the same period. (How much of this is our biases and how much of it is real?) So the standard Enlightenment narrative goes very rougly something like "starts in Italy, moves north into Switzerland, the low countries, and France, eventually reaches its pinnacle in England and Germany." You see approximately the same narrative in the transition from medieval to early modern philosophy: start with the Rome-associated Scholastics, move north into France with Descartes, Malebranche, etc. and end up in England and Germany with Locke, Leibniz, Hume, Kant, Mill, Hegel. So the question I would ask would be something like: why does Spain get left out of both of these stories?
